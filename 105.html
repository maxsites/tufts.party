COMP 105 Assignment: uParty

Due Friday, December 16, at 10:00PM.

The purpose of this assignment is to help you get acquainted with your fellow classmates after a strugglebus of a semester. The assignment is divided into two parts.

  • In the first part, you may submit a request for some non-negative number of modules for use in this assignment. Most find this helpful, but for some it is unnecessary.
  • In the second part, you implement uParty at the specified location.

Individual Problems

Working on your own, please solve Exercise M.

Problem Details

If you would prefer not to request a module, please skip this problem.

Exercise M. Request a module for use in uParty. The form to request modules for this assignment can be found here. You will need to provide the request form with your Venmo username.

For students without a Venmo account, write the username NONE. Students without Venmo accounts are expected to bring cash to the door.

Notes:

  • Option NONE is special in its capitalization.

  • Failing to pay for your module soon after you are charged results in exception BringYourOwn being raised.

  • All non-notable requests must go through the module request form. No exceptions.

Group Problems

For these problems, you are encouraged to work with a partner or a group.

Problem Details

Sometimes you want to de-stress after the final exam of a difficult course, ideally with other students who just finished the same course. In this portion of the homework, you will implement uParty in several stages.

Exercise 1. Get letrec'd. How else will you modify your environment?

Exercise 2. (timesRepeat: 105 {(dance)}). What is a good uParty implementation without some dancing? Get out there, go crazy.

Hints and Guidelines

  • Submit your module request early. Module requests made closer to the party are less likely to get fulfilled.

  • For your reference, this critically-acclaimed documentary about partying and social interaction discusses relevant topics at length. It should be free online via questionable means. You may also find the geek's guide to college parties infographic helpful.

  • Avoid Smalltalk. The goal of this assignment is to have a good time, not to awkwardly stand around chatting. Do not talk about the weather or how poorly the exam went!

  • We do not provide any invariants or guarantees as to behavior following the termination of the party environment.

  • uParty may not implement garbage collection, so please be efficient with your use of modules.

  • Norman Ramsey's implementation of this assignment takes two modules and has been lit since 1990.
